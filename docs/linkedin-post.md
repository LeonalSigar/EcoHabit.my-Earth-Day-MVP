EcoHabit.my is a small Earth Day web app I built for the DEV Weekend Challenge.

The goal was to make climate action feel practical instead of generic:

- take LRT/MRT instead of solo Grab
- tapau lunch in your own bekas
- bring a tumbler for kopi or teh
- set the AC to 25°C instead of overcooling the room

I wanted it to feel specifically Malaysian in texture, but still readable to a global audience.

Built with React, TypeScript, Vite, and localStorage. No backend, no auth, plus a seeded anonymous pledge wall so the product feels alive on first load.

It also includes a personalized action prompt and a poster-style share card with a QR code back to the live app.

What I liked most about building this was the constraint: under a short deadline, the real challenge was not adding features, but choosing the right ones.
